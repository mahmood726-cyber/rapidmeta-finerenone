### ENTRY P1: FIDELITY Pooled Analysis (Agarwal et al. 2022)

**Citation:** Agarwal R, Filippatos G, Pitt B, Anker SD, Rossing P, Joseph A, et al. Cardiovascular and kidney outcomes with finerenone in patients with type 2 diabetes and chronic kidney disease: the FIDELITY pooled analysis. European Heart Journal. 2022;43(6):474-484.
- **PMID:** 35023547
- **DOI:** [10.1093/eurheartj/ehab777](https://doi.org/10.1093/eurheartj/ehab777)

**Type:** Pre-specified individual patient-level pooled analysis (NOT a literature-based meta-analysis)

**Trials Included (2):** FIDELIO-DKD + FIGARO-DKD
**Total Patients:** 13,026
**Population:** T2D + CKD (broad spectrum)
**Median Follow-up:** 3.0 years (IQR 2.3-3.8)

**Pooled Results:**

| Outcome | Measure | Estimate | 95% CI | P-value |
|---------|---------|----------|--------|---------|
| CV composite (CV death/MI/stroke/HHF) | HR | 0.86 | 0.78-0.95 | 0.0018 |
| Kidney composite (kidney failure/sustained >=57% eGFR decrease/renal death) | HR | 0.77 | 0.67-0.88 | 0.0002 |
| HF hospitalization | HR | 0.78 | 0.66-0.92 | NR |
| Hyperkalemia leading to discontinuation | % | 1.7% vs 0.6% | -- | -- |

**Effect Measure:** Hazard Ratio (individual patient-level time-to-event)
**Model:** Pre-specified pooled IPD analysis (not standard meta-analysis model)
**Heterogeneity:** N/A (IPD pooled, not study-level MA)

## KEY OBSERVATIONS

1. **Consistent MACE reduction**: All meta-analyses show MACE/CV composite HR/RR in the range of 0.81-0.88, universally statistically significant.

2. **All-cause mortality**: Most analyses show 8-14% reduction (RR/HR 0.86-0.94), with larger studies (including FINEARTS-HF) reaching significance. Pre-FINEARTS analyses (4-trial designs) often borderline non-significant.

3. **CV death**: Consistently fails to reach significance when FINEARTS-HF is included (HR ~0.89-0.93). Yasmin 2023 (using HR from 7 smaller trials) found significant reduction (HR 0.84, 0.74-0.95).

4. **HF hospitalization**: Universally significant across all analyses, with 18-22% reduction (HR/RR 0.78-0.82).

5. **Kidney composite**: Strong and consistent 16-23% reduction (HR 0.77-0.84), always significant.

6. **Hyperkalemia**: Consistently ~2-fold increased risk (RR 2.03-2.31), though rarely requiring hospitalization and no hyperkalemia-related deaths reported.

7. **Overall adverse events**: Universally no difference from placebo (RR ~1.00).
