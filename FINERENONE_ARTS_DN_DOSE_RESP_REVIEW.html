Methods (click to expand)

This review re-analyzes the within-trial dose-response of a single Phase 2b dose-finding RCT of finerenone in adults with type-2 diabetic nephropathy (Bakris GL et al., JAMA 2015). Patients were randomized to once-daily oral finerenone 1.25 – 20 mg or matching placebo for 90 days on top of background ACEi or ARB. The registered primary outcome was the placebo-corrected ratio of Day-90 UACR to baseline UACR. The trial was conducted at 148 sites in 23 countries and 821 of 823 randomized patients received study drug.

Why k=1 (and why we ship it): ARTS-DN is the only finerenone dose-finding trial that contributes a within-design dose-response curve to the finerenone evidence base. The downstream Phase 3 programme (FIDELIO-DKD, FIGARO-DKD, FINEARTS-HF) tested only the 10 – 20 mg dose schedule. A two-trial or larger pooled within-drug dose-response requires multiple Phase 2 dose-finders, which does not exist for finerenone. The engine's k=1 path (PR #270, v0.4) was specifically added to surface within-trial dose-response inference honestly for designs like this.

Estimand choice: The fixture uses the AACT-registered primary outcome (Day-90 UACR ratio, on-treatment, LSM with 90% CI on the multiplicative-ratio scale). SE on the log scale is derived from the 90% CI half-width via SE_log = (log_CI_hi − log_CI_lo) / (2 × 1.6449); per-arm SD on the log scale is then SD = SE × √n. These are the engine's continuous-outcome inputs.

Statistical methods match the engine v0.3.0 + v0.4 k=1 single-trial branch:

  • Linear pool (Tab N/A, dropped): Engine fitLinear requires k ≥ 2 and throws on k=1 by design. The RCS linear-basis component (spline_coefs[0]) serves the linear-effect summary directly. Adding a k=1 path to fitLinear is deferred (no clear use case beyond duplicating spline_coefs[0]).
  • RCS pool (Tab 1, HEADLINE): 3-knot restricted cubic spline at Harrell defaults (10th / 50th / 90th percentile of unique non-zero doses ⇒ 3.75 / 7.5 / 12.5 mg on this dose grid). Kp = 2 spline coefficients. spline_coefs[0] is the linear-basis dimension and spline_coefs[1] is the non-linear-basis dimension. For k=1 the engine returns τ2_matrix = zeros(Kp, Kp), τ2_per_dim = [0, 0], hksj_mv = 1 (no inflation), q_mv = 0, df_mv = max(1, n_arms − Kp) = 6, tcrit = qt(0.975, n_arms − Kp − 1) = qt(0.975, 5) = 2.571. The pooled coefficient covariance cov_beta equals the trial's own V; SEs are the diagonals of that matrix. The Wald non-linearity test is computed on the same cov_beta.
  • One-stage hierarchical (Tab N/A, dropped): R lme4::lmer(mean ~ dose + (1 | studlab), ...) requires ≥ 2 sampled grouping levels for the random study intercept to identify variance; at k=1 it raises "grouping factors must have > 1 sampled level". The R precompute confirms this (one_stage.fit_ok = false; error_msg = "grouping factors must have > 1 sampled level"). Not applicable at k=1; not a deficiency of the engine.
  • R-parity badge (Tab 2): The standard 5-row badge from vendor/r-validation-doseresp.js assumes both engine linear (k ≥ 2) and one-stage (k ≥ 2) layers. At k=1 the engine has no linear output and no one_stage output. The R precompute does return linear.pooled_slope_log from R dosresmeta (which has its own k=1 path) and rcs.spline_coefs[0..1] from R dosresmeta RCS — so RCS-level engine-vs-R comparison is possible. We render an abbreviated 2-row R-parity panel for the RCS-only comparison plus a deferral notice for the absent linear and one-stage rows. Full-badge restoration is deferred to engine v0.5 (which would require adapting mixmeta for k=1, currently k ≥ 2 only).

Continuous-outcome support: Engine v0.2.0+ continuous branch in fitRCS uses a per-trial covariance matrix mirroring the binary GL shared-reference structure (off-diagonal = sdref2/nref; diagonal = sdi2/ni + sdref2/nref). Per-arm SDs derived from AACT-reported SE via SD = SE × √n.
