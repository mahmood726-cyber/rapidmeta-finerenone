Methods (click to expand)

This review pools 4 Phase 3 RCTs of upadacitinib in adults with moderate-to-severe rheumatoid arthritis, all from the AbbVie SELECT programme (2018-2020). All trials randomized to once-daily oral extended-release upadacitinib vs a control (placebo for the two IR trials with csDMARD background; methotrexate for the two monotherapy trials), measured DAS28-CRP change-from-baseline at 12 or 14 weeks, and were registered prospectively on ClinicalTrials.gov.

Within-drug dose-response: All 4 trials use the same molecule (upadacitinib) and same route (extended-release oral once daily). The pooled slope is therefore a within-drug DAS28-CRP dose-response curve and has no cross-drug equipotency assumption. The 15 and 30 mg dose levels span the upadacitinib RA dosing range studied in Phase 3 (the 15 mg dose is the FDA-approved RA dose; 30 mg was studied as the high-dose arm). Two trials (NEXT, BEYOND) contribute the 0→15 mg transition (placebo as dose=0); the remaining two (MONOTHERAPY, EARLY) drop the active comparator and contribute only the 15→30 mg increment.

Active-comparator handling (Option A — drop, do not down-code as dose=0): SELECT-MONOTHERAPY and SELECT-EARLY randomized upadacitinib vs methotrexate (active comparator). Methotrexate arms are DROPPED from the dose-response arms list — the engine treats the lowest upadacitinib dose (15 mg) as the per-trial reference (is_reference=true) for those 2 trials. SELECT-NEXT and SELECT-BEYOND were placebo-controlled, so placebo is the reference (dose=0). The dropped-comparator metadata (comparator_label, comparator_lsm_change, comparator_ci, comparator_n, _dropped_comparator_arm) is preserved per-trial in the fixture as an audit trail.

Engine RCS-fallback (the headline methodological story): The 3-knot Harrell-default RCS positions knots at the 10th, 50th, and 90th percentiles of the unique positive doses. With only 3 distinct positive doses in the pool ({15, 30} mg from MONOTHERAPY / EARLY plus the implicit reference doses already accounted for), the engine's rcsKnots(allDoses, 3) returns fewer than the required K=3 distinct knot locations, and fitRCS short-circuits to a call to fitLinear on the same input, decorating the result with layer='linear', fallback='degenerate_to_linear', and rcs=null. R dosresmeta refuses to fit RCS on the same fixture with the error “A two-stage approach requires that each study provides at least p non-referent obs” — engine and R agree the dose grid does not support a 3-knot RCS in two-stage form. The linear two-stage layer (k=4) is unaffected and is the methodologically primary result for this fixture.

Estimand uniformity: Treatment-policy / treatment-regimen MMRM with multiple imputation per protocol-specified statistical analysis plan used consistently across all 4 trials for cross-trial comparability. AACT does not register split treatment-regimen vs trial-product estimands for these SELECT continuous outcomes — only one estimand row per result_group per outcome_id. The Full Analysis Set with multiple imputation population is used in all 4 trials.

Dispersion-convention note: AACT registers LEAST_SQUARES_MEAN with dispersion_type='95% Confidence Interval' (NOT Standard Error) for all 4 SELECT DAS28-CRP outcomes. The fixture back-computes SE = (CI_upper − CI_lower) / (2 × 1.96), then SD = SE × √n using the per-arm analyzed N from outcome_counts.txt. Raw CI bounds preserved per arm as _aact_ci_lower / _aact_ci_upper. The engine's mdCovariance() uses sd2/n which equals SE2 — invariant to this re-expression.

SELECT-BEYOND non-monotonicity at 30 mg (real, published, not an extraction error): In the bDMARD-IR refractory population SELECT-BEYOND reports DAS28-CRP LSM change at Week 12 of placebo -1.02, upa 15 mg -2.31, upa 30 mg -2.29 — the 30 mg arm is 0.02 percentage points LESS negative than the 15 mg arm (overlapping 95% CIs [-2.52, -2.10] vs [-2.50, -2.09]). This bDMARD-IR plateau matches the Genovese 2018 Lancet abstract (“both doses of upadacitinib led to rapid and significant improvements” without a 30 mg margin over 15 mg). The per-trial GL slope for SELECT-BEYOND is therefore -0.042 per mg (still negative, identified by the 0→15 mg leg, slightly attenuated by the 15→30 mg plateau). Engine fitLinear handles non-monotonic per-trial contrasts correctly — the pooled slope is identified from across-trial covariation, not from within-trial monotonicity. Documented in fixture monotonicity_audit_2026_05_14.

Statistical methods match the engine v0.4 conventions: two-stage Greenland-Longnecker linear pool with REML+HKSJ (HKSJ floor max(1, Q/(k-1))); Q-profile τ2 CI (Viechtbauer 2007); PI uses tk-1 df (Cochrane Handbook v6.5). The RCS layer is requested but the engine returns the documented fallback (layer='linear', fallback='degenerate_to_linear', no .rcs block) because the 3-distinct-dose grid does not support a 3-knot Harrell basis under two-stage pooling. R dosresmeta linear layer matches the engine within |Δ| < threshold; R RCS refused with a parallel sparse-arm error; the R-parity badge therefore reduces to the linear-pool rows only.
