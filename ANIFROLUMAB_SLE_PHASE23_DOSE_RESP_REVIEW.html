Methods (click to expand)

This review pools 3 RCTs of anifrolumab in adults with moderate-to-severe SLE, all from the AstraZeneca anifrolumab program (2017–2020). All trials randomized to anifrolumab IV every 4 weeks for 48 weeks of treatment, measured BICLA response at Week 52, and were registered prospectively on ClinicalTrials.gov.

Within-drug dose-response: All 3 trials use the same molecule (anifrolumab, a human monoclonal antibody to type I interferon receptor subunit 1), same route (intravenous infusion), same dosing interval (every 4 weeks for 48 weeks of treatment), same endpoint (BICLA composite at Week 52), and same indication (adults with moderate-to-severe SLE on stable standard-of-care). MUSE is Phase 2b (305 randomized) whereas TULIP-1 and TULIP-2 are Phase 3 (457 and 362 randomized respectively). Pooled slope is a within-drug BICLA dose-response curve with no cross-drug equipotency assumption. The 150 mg and 300 mg arms are the Phase 3 doses tested in TULIP-1 (300 mg ultimately the FDA-approved dose used in TULIP-2); the 1000 mg arm is the Phase 2b high-dose probe that demonstrated diminishing returns relative to 300 mg in MUSE.

Active-comparator handling (Option A — not triggered): No active comparator arms exist in any of the 3 trials. All 3 trials are placebo-controlled and placebo is retained as the dose=0 reference. Option A drops are not required and the dropped-arm audit metadata in the fixture is empty.

Binary-outcome path (engine v0.3.0): The engine's fitLinear takes per-arm events and n directly and constructs the Greenland-Longnecker shared-reference covariance for log-RR contrasts (off-diagonal = 1/eventref − 1/nref; diagonal = 1/eventi − 1/ni + 1/eventref − 1/nref). No SE→SD conversion is needed (unlike the continuous-outcome flagships SGLT2I / TIRZEPATIDE / SUSTAIN / SELECT / ERENUMAB). F-1 conditional zero-cell continuity correction is wired in the engine but does not fire on this fixture (every arm has > 0 BICLA responders; the smallest event count is MUSE placebo with 26/102 events). Linear-pool inputs: k=3 trials, 8 arm observations including 3 reference arms.

Engine RCS fit (the headline — full multivariate REML succeeds at k_RCS=2): The 3-knot Harrell-default RCS positions knots at the 10th, 50th, and 90th percentiles of the unique positive doses across the pool. With 3 distinct positive doses ({150, 300, 1000} mg) the engine's rcsKnots returns 3 distinct knot locations (engine output: knots [225, 300, 650] mg). fitRCS then attempts a per-trial Kp×Kp=2×2 spline design fit; TULIP-2 has only 1 non-reference arm so its per-trial XtSX is singular and the engine silently drops TULIP-2 from the RCS pool (k_RCS=2 surviving trials: MUSE + TULIP-1). Full multivariate REML (Nelder-Mead on Cholesky parameters of the 2×2 τ2 matrix) converges in 32 iterations to a log-likelihood of −9.36; HKSJ-multivariate floor activates at 1.0 (Qmv < dfmv); t-critical at df=k_RCS−1=1 is 12.706 (very wide intervals reflecting k_RCS=2). The engine's RCS curve at the key program doses (Tab 2): est 0.000 at dose=0 (reference), 0.253 at dose=150 mg, 0.583 at dose=300 mg, 7.622 at dose=1000 mg. The 0→300 mg leg captures the active-dose-range steepness; the 300→1000 mg leg reflects the engine extrapolating past where TULIP-1 supports the slope (MUSE alone constrains 300→1000 mg). Read Tab 2 for the full 20-point grid and the saturation interpretation.

Why R refuses (sparse-per-trial-arm requirement): R dosresmeta's two-stage RCS engine requires each trial to supply ≥ Kp=2 non-reference observations (because the per-trial 2×2 spline-coefficient regression is otherwise underdetermined). TULIP-2 has only 1 non-reference arm (300 mg only), so R refuses with the canonical error “A two-stage approach requires that each study provides at least p non-referent obs (p is the number of columns of the design matrix X)”. R does not silently drop the offending trial; the entire RCS pool is refused. The R precompute records rcs.fit_ok = false and the error message verbatim. This is the same R refusal pattern as SUSTAIN (Round 3.7), SELECT (Round 3.8), and ERENUMAB (Round 3.10) — but UNLIKE SUSTAIN, the engine here produces an interpretable RCS fit because TULIP-2 is the only sparse-arm trial (MUSE and TULIP-1 both have 2 non-reference arms each; k_RCS=2 survives), whereas SUSTAIN had 4 of 6 trials drop and the surviving k_RCS=2 was dominated by a single SUSTAIN-FORTE 1→2 mg leg.

Why this is methodologically richer than AMAGINE (Round 3.9): AMAGINE's engine-vs-R disagreement had the OPPOSITE direction (engine refused, R fit), and R's knots all collapsed inside the 140–210 mg interval where there were no interior data points. Here the engine fits and R refuses, AND the engine's knots [225, 300, 650] mg span the actual dose range with meaningful gaps (225–300 mg = 75 mg apart, 300–650 mg = 350 mg apart). The engine's RCS captures the program-canonical saturation finding (MUSE 300 mg 53.5% > 1000 mg 41.3% BICLA response — the empirical evidence that 300 mg is the optimal anifrolumab dose, and the reason TULIP-1/TULIP-2 dropped the 1000 mg arm). The non-linearity Wald p = 0.60 reflects that the saturation evidence is under-leveraged at k_RCS=2 with only MUSE supplying the 1000 mg arm; the engine can fit the curve but the formal test cannot reject H0 of linearity. A future Phase 4 trial with multiple anifrolumab doses including 1000 mg would tighten this Wald test.

Estimand uniformity (treatment-policy with non-responder imputation): All 3 trials use the FDA-pre-registered treatment-policy estimand with NRI for missing data (discontinuations and protocol-restricted-medication use both count as non-response). For TULIP-1 the fixture uses the pre-specified BICLA analysis (outcome 211061110) NOT the post-hoc-revised analysis (outcome 211061111) — this preserves estimand-uniformity with TULIP-2's pre-specified BICLA primary. The BICLA composite definition (reduction of all baseline BILAG-2004 A scores to B/C/D, no new BILAG-2004 A or 2+ new BILAG-2004 B, no SLEDAI-2K worsening, no PGA worsening ≥0.3, no IP discontinuation, no restricted-medication use beyond threshold) is identical across the 3 trials.

Statistical methods match engine v0.3.0 conventions: two-stage Greenland-Longnecker linear pool with REML + HKSJ (HKSJ floor max(1, Q/(k-1))); restricted cubic splines with 3 Harrell-default knots; full multivariate REML for the RCS τ2 matrix (Nelder-Mead on Cholesky parameters); HKSJ-multivariate + tk−1 CIs on RCS pooled output; Q-profile τ2 CI on fitLinear (Viechtbauer 2007); R lme4::glmer one-stage Poisson/Binomial-with-offset computed by R and surfaced from precomputed JSON. PI uses tk−1 df (Cochrane Handbook v6.5).
