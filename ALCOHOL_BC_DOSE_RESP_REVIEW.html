Alcohol intake and breast cancer incidence — dose-response meta-analysis

Pooled re-analysis of Greenland & Longnecker 1992 worked example, Am J Epidemiol 135:1301-9 (PMID 1626547).

Authors: RapidMeta dose-response engine pack #4 · Generated by: … · Date: …

Methods (click to expand)

This review applies three dose-response meta-analysis fitters in parallel:

  1. Two-stage Greenland-Longnecker linear pool — per-study log-linear slope via weighted least squares with the GL covariance correction, then REML+HKSJ pool. Prediction interval uses tk−1 df (Cochrane Handbook v6.5 §10.10.4.3). HKSJ floor max(1, Q/(k−1)) per advanced-stats.md.
  2. Two-stage GL + restricted cubic splines — 3 knots at Harrell percentiles (25/50/75 of unique non-reference doses); multivariate-RE pool with diagonal-PM τ² approximation; Wald joint non-linearity test.
  3. One-stage Poisson hierarchical — lme4::glmer(events ~ dose + (1 | studlab), offset = log(n), family = poisson) computed by R, surfaced as a precomputed-display layer (no JS fitter).

Engine validation: parity against R dosresmeta::dosresmeta(method="reml") on the linear component (|Δ| < 0.01) and against lme4::glmer on one-stage (precomputed). Non-linearity Wald p diverges from R by design — see the R-parity tab.

Dose assignment: values in the fixture (0, 1.5, 5, 15, 20, 24, 30, 45 g/day) are assigned midpoints or open-ended category typical values as tabulated in GL 1992 Table 1; they are not category boundaries.
