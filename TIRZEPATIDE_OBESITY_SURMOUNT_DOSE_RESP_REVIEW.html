Pooled within-drug dose-response re-analysis of 2 SURMOUNT Phase 3 RCTs of tirzepatide (5, 10, 15 mg once weekly) on percent body-weight change at 72 weeks: SURMOUNT-1 (PMID 35658024) · SURMOUNT-2 (PMID 37385275).

Small-k stress-test flagship (k=2): This flagship is intentionally built on only 2 trials. Its purpose is to demonstrate how the engine v0.3.0 behaves at the small-k boundary of its HKSJ floor max(1, Q/(k-1)) and at the k=2 limit of the tk-1 critical value t1, 0.975 = 12.706. CIs are honestly wide; HKSJ-multivariate scaling is large (engine = 19.5) because Q is enormous on this 2-trial sample; the engine's Paule-Mandel τ2 diverges from R REML by ~0.002 (an estimator-divergence that closes at k≥5). Do NOT cite the linear pool point estimate as a real-world obesity meta-analytic effect. Use the RCS Tab 2 for shape interpretation and Tab 4 for the documented PM-vs-REML τ2 divergence.

Methods (click to expand)

This review pools 2 Phase 3 RCTs of tirzepatide in adults with obesity (with and without T2D), both from the SURMOUNT programme (Eli Lilly, 2022-2023). Both trials randomized to once-weekly SC tirzepatide vs placebo over a 72-week dosing schedule (including a 20-week dose escalation), measured percent body-weight change from baseline as the primary outcome, and were registered prospectively on ClinicalTrials.gov.

Active-comparator handling (Not applicable): Both SURMOUNT trials are placebo-controlled. Placebo is the per-trial dose=0 reference. There are no active-comparator arms to drop, unlike the Round 3 SURPASS T2D flagship which had 3 active-comparator trials.

SURMOUNT-2 lacks a 5 mg arm: The dose-response grid in this fixture has 3 levels (5/10/15 mg) but only SURMOUNT-1 contributes information at 5 mg. SURMOUNT-2 contributes at 10 and 15 mg only. The engine handles this transparently via the per-trial arms list — the two-stage linear pool computes a per-trial Greenland-Longnecker slope from each trial's own arms, then pools the slopes; the RCS pool fits per-trial spline coefficients on each trial's own dose grid.

Estimand choice (AACT efficacy estimand, differs from SURPASS): The SURMOUNT fixture uses the AACT-registered efficacy estimand (on-treatment, excluding data after prematurely stopping study drug). The PubMed abstracts of the primary publications report a treatment-regimen estimand instead (which assesses effects regardless of treatment discontinuation in the intention-to-treat population). For SURMOUNT-1 the efficacy values are -16.0 / -21.4 / -22.5 vs -2.4 (5/10/15 mg vs placebo) whereas the NEJM abstract reports -15.0 / -19.5 / -20.9 vs -3.1 (treatment-regimen). For SURMOUNT-2 the efficacy values are -13.4 / -15.7 vs -3.3 (10/15 mg vs placebo) whereas the Lancet abstract reports -12.8 / -14.7 vs -3.2 (treatment-regimen). Both estimands are pre-registered and FDA-acceptable. The AACT efficacy estimand was used here for source-of-truth uniformity within the fixture (same convention as the data-extraction agent's documented decision in fixture _comment and per-trial data_provenance). The Round 3 SURPASS flagship instead used the AACT treatment-regimen estimand because that was the AACT default for HbA1c outcomes at the time of extraction — the across-flagship estimand inconsistency is a fixture-specific source-of-truth choice, not an engine choice.

k=2 small-k caveat (prominent): This is the flagship's central methodological point. With only 2 trials, the engine's tk-1 critical value collapses to t1, 0.975 = 12.706 for linear-pool CIs (vs ~2 for k=10). The HKSJ floor max(1, Q/(k-1)) is at its degenerate small-k boundary (denominator k-1 = 1). Q on this fixture is ~61 in the linear layer and ~58 in the multivariate RCS layer, yielding an HKSJ-mv scaling of ~19.5 — honest reflection that 2 trials with very different baseline-population characteristics (obesity-without-T2D at heavier 104.8 kg mean weight vs obesity-with-T2D at lighter 100.7 kg mean weight, with T2D-related glycaemic confounding) cannot pin down a within-drug dose-response curve. The 3-knot RCS at k=2 with Kp=2 spline coefficients is barely identifiable; the REML fit converges but the inference is power-limited (compare to SURPASS at k=5 where the same Wald non-linearity test produced p=0.0346 vs here p=0.0759 — the qualitative saturation is the same, the power is lower).

Statistical methods match the engine v0.3.0 conventions: two-stage Greenland-Longnecker linear pool with HKSJ (fitLinear uses Paule-Mandel τ2 per lessons.md "Never use DL for k<10 — use REML or PM"); HKSJ floor max(1, Q/(k-1)); restricted cubic splines with 3 Harrell-default knots (placed at the 10th, 50th, 90th percentiles of the unique non-zero doses, which collapses to 7.5 / 10 / 12.5 mg on this dose grid); full multivariate REML for the RCS τ2 matrix (Nelder-Mead on Cholesky parameters); HKSJ-multivariate + tk-1 CIs on RCS pooled output; Q-profile τ2 CI on fitLinear (Viechtbauer 2007); one-stage hierarchical (percent body-weight change, continuous, lme4::lmer with bobyqa optimizer) computed by R and surfaced from precomputed JSON. PI uses tk-1 df (Cochrane Handbook v6.5). Note on the PM-vs-REML τ2 divergence: at k=2 the engine's PM τ2 (0.0997) differs from R's REML τ2 (0.0977) by |Δ| = 0.0020. This exceeds the badge threshold of 0.0001 and lights the R-parity row AMBER. Both estimators are valid; PM and REML converge for k≥5. The AMBER row is expected and documented in Tab 4 below.

Continuous-outcome support: Engine v0.2.0+ continuous branch in fitLinear/fitRCS uses a per-trial covariance matrix mirroring the binary GL shared-reference structure (off-diagonal = sdref2/nref; diagonal = sdi2/ni + sdref2/nref). Per-arm SDs derived from AACT-reported SE via sd = se × √n. Validated against R dosresmeta(type="md") in the R-parity badge tab (4 of 5 rows GREEN; linear_tau2 AMBER by design).

R-parity badge (engine vs R)

Five threshold-driven rows comparing engine v0.3.0 output against R dosresmeta + R mixmeta (RCS non-linearity) on the same 2-trial SURMOUNT fixture. 4 of 5 rows are expected GREEN; the linear_tau2 row is expected AMBER — this is the engine's PM τ2 diverging from R's REML τ2 at k=2 by ~0.002 (more than the 0.0001 threshold). Both estimators are valid; PM and REML converge for k≥5. See the disclosure below the badge for details.

Why is the linear_tau2 row AMBER? The engine's fitLinear uses the Paule-Mandel (PM) τ2 estimator (per lessons.md rule: "Never use DL for k<10 — use REML or PM"). The R precompute uses REML via dosresmeta. At k=2 these two estimators legitimately diverge by ~0.002 on this fixture (engine PM 0.0997, R REML 0.0977). The R-parity badge's threshold for linear_tau2 is tight (0.0001) and is calibrated for k≥5 where PM and REML converge. This AMBER row is expected at k=2 and is not a bug. Both estimators are statistically valid; the divergence reflects estimator behaviour at the small-k boundary, exactly the kind of small-k engine-stress demonstration this flagship is built for. The fitRCS layer (rows 3-5) uses full multivariate REML and matches R within |Δ| < 0.01 on every row.
