Methods (click to expand)

This review pools 5 Phase 3 RCTs of tirzepatide in adults with type 2 diabetes (T2D), all from the SURPASS programme (Eli Lilly, 2021-2022). All trials randomized to once-weekly SC tirzepatide at 5, 10, or 15 mg vs a control (placebo or active comparator), measured HbA1c change-from-baseline at 40 or 52 weeks, and were registered prospectively on ClinicalTrials.gov.

Within-drug dose-response (no class-equivalence caveat): Unlike the Round 2A SGLT2i flagship, which pools 3 different drugs at the raw-mg scale, this flagship pools 5 RCTs of the SAME molecule (tirzepatide) at the SAME 3 dose levels (5/10/15 mg). The pooled slope is therefore a within-drug dose-response curve and has no cross-drug equipotency assumption. The only inter-trial heterogeneity contributors are background therapy (drug-naive vs metformin vs insulin-glargine-bg), follow-up window (40 vs 52 weeks), and population CV risk.

Active-comparator handling (Option A — drop, do not down-code as dose=0): SURPASS-2 / SURPASS-3 / SURPASS-4 randomized tirzepatide vs an active comparator (semaglutide 1 mg, insulin degludec, insulin glargine respectively). Active comparator arms are DROPPED from the dose-response arms list — the engine treats the lowest tirzepatide dose (5 mg) as the per-trial reference (is_reference=true) for those 3 trials. SURPASS-1 and SURPASS-5 were placebo-controlled, so placebo is the reference (dose=0). The comparator metadata (comparator, comparator_label, comparator_lsm_change_pct) is preserved in the trial-level fixture as the audit trail; see active_comparator_handling in the fixture JSON. The alternative — treating an active comparator as "dose=0" — would conflate the comparator effect with placebo and bias the pooled slope toward zero.

Estimand choice (AACT treatment-regimen estimand): SURPASS-2 / SURPASS-4 / SURPASS-5 abstracts report a different (efficacy) estimand than the AACT-registered (treatment-regimen) estimand. For cross-trial consistency, this flagship uses the AACT treatment-regimen estimand uniformly across all 5 trials. The difference is small (typically <0.3 percentage points at 15 mg) and does not change the qualitative dose-response shape or the non-linearity finding.

Dose-saturation finding: Engine RCS analysis returns non-linearity Wald p = 0.0346 (full multivariate REML v0.3.0; R mixmeta reports p = 0.0364, |Δ| = 0.002). This is the headline finding: tirzepatide HbA1c response is not linear in mg-dose — the curve saturates after approximately 5 mg, with the 5-to-10 mg increment producing about 2-3 times more HbA1c reduction per mg than the 10-to-15 mg increment. The linear two-stage pool (Tab 1) therefore underestimates the 5 mg effect and overestimates the 15 mg incremental effect; the RCS framing (Tab 2) is the methodologically correct primary analysis.

Statistical methods match the engine v0.3.0 conventions: two-stage Greenland-Longnecker linear pool with REML+HKSJ (HKSJ floor max(1, Q/(k-1))); restricted cubic splines with 3 Harrell-default knots; full multivariate REML for the RCS τ² matrix (Nelder-Mead on Cholesky parameters); HKSJ-multivariate + tk-1 CIs on RCS pooled output; Q-profile τ² CI on fitLinear (Viechtbauer 2007); one-stage hierarchical (HbA1c, continuous, lme4::lmer with bobyqa optimizer) computed by R and surfaced from precomputed JSON. PI uses tk-1 df (Cochrane Handbook v6.5).

Continuous-outcome support: Engine v0.2.0+ continuous branch in fitLinear/fitRCS uses a per-trial covariance matrix mirroring the binary GL shared-reference structure (off-diagonal = sdref2/nref; diagonal = sdi2/ni + sdref2/nref). Per-arm SDs derived from AACT-reported SE via sd = se × √n. Validated against R dosresmeta(type="md") in the R-parity badge tab.

Data provenance: AACT snapshot 2026-04-12, outcome_measurements.txt + outcome_counts.txt. All per-arm LSM change-from-baseline and SE values are direct from the AACT registry-deposited efficacy outcome (primary outcome for 10/15 mg, key-secondary for 5 mg — both with identical time_frame and definition, a regulatory multiplicity-testing artefact, not a different measurement).
